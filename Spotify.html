Stuck in the Loop of Familiar Tune

When I moved to Madison from India, I switched from using local music apps to Spotify, encouraged by friends eager to share their playlists. At first, I was thrilled—Spotify seemed to get my taste instantly, curating music that felt spot on. But after a while, I started feeling stuck. The app kept showing me more of what I already knew, and when I wanted to explore new genres or discover different artists, it fell short. I wondered if other users were hitting the same wall, hence started my journey of uncovering deeper user insights on Spotify.

Problem

The objective of our study was not just to find ways to boost user engagement and enhance the user experience for Spotify but to also empower the platform’s future through user-centric insights.

"What are the overarching user experience challenges faced by Spotify users, and how can these insights inform strategies for enhancing overall satisfaction with the platform?"

‍

White Paper Research

The success of the company can be credited to its “freemium” approach, where users are encouraged to transition from free to premium memberships, enjoying perks like offline playlists, improved sound quality (320 kbps bitrate), and an ad-free experience.

Spotify’s freemium model provides dual benefits to the company. Free subscriptions — populated with advertisements — bring people through the door, while premium subscriptions bring in recurring revenue. While the company has historically had better revenue growth and better margins on the premium side, Vogel said, at least 60% of subscribers have come on board to Spotify by signing up first for a free subscription.

Competitor Analysis

Spotify faces robust competition from industry heavyweights such as Apple Music, Amazon Music, YouTube Music, Tencent Music, and Pandora.

While Spotify’s popularity is driven by user experience and affordability, rivals like Apple Music boast expansive libraries, and Amazon Music leverages its e-commerce dominance.

YouTube Music is grappling with slower digital space progress, Tencent Music dominates the Chinese market, and Pandora faces challenges in global expansion.

However, Spotify maintains a competitive edge through its extensive content library and aggressive user base expansion strategies, navigating the dynamic landscape of the music streaming industry.

Mixed Method Research

We conducted User Surveys, User Interviews and triangulated our research using by Contextual Inquiries. We aimed at gathering both qualitative and quantitative insights: surveys provided broad behavioral data, interviews offered in-depth user-specific feedback on key features, and contextual inquiries enabled direct observation of user interactions, ensuring a holistic understanding of Spotify's UX challenges.

Research Target Audience

  • Current Spotify users, to understand their loyalty and expectations.
  • Users who have transitioned from Spotify to competitors to uncover the reasons behind their switch.
  • Users of competing music streaming platforms to gauge their experiences and preferences.
  • Potential users who are considering different platforms, offering insights into what they seek in a music streaming service.

#1 User Interviews

Participants — 6

The Process — For the interviews, we employed a semi-structured format, exploring music discovery, playlist management, and UI navigation. With the goal being, gathering qualitative insights, fostering a nuanced understanding of user engagement and informing improvements to the Spotify experience.

Strategy — We have used convenience and purposeful sampling: asking friends, family, and colleagues in our network about music platforms. Also, engaging diverse perspectives by reaching out to online communities like music forums, Reddit, and social media groups. Users’ intrinsic motivation drove survey participation for our academic research. We made sure that we included the extreme users, i.e, users of other platforms.

Insights — Personalization, playlist management, and social engagement are crucial in music streaming. Users prioritize platforms that tailor content, foster social interactions, and offer efficient playlist management. Discoverability and dynamic playlist curation are key, emphasizing the role of platforms like Spotify in shaping individual musical journeys.

How Might We?

We distilled our design principles into three core elements aimed at enhancing diversity in Spotify's curated playlists. The focus is on inclusivity, streamlining the user interface for ease of use, and boosting social features to foster community engagement, benefiting users like Julia and Michael.

1. How might we elevate the diversity of curated playlists to better match individual preferences?
2.How might we streamline navigation for an intuitive user experience?
3. How might we deepen social features to foster meaningful user engagement and connections?
Proposal

This proposal addresses key pain points to enhance Spotify’s user experience and meet diverse user needs.



Elevate Curated Playlists-

  • Enhance the diversity of curated playlists to better match individual preferences.
  • Introduce algorithmic improvements to tailor playlists based on a user’s evolving music taste.
  • Implement user feedback mechanisms to allow direct input on playlist preferences.


Streamline Navigation-

  • Simplify the user interface and navigation for an effortless and intuitive experience.
  • Introduce personalized dashboards allowing quick access to frequently used features.
  • Optimize the search function for more accurate and efficient content discovery.


Empower Music Creators-

  • Improve discoverability for aspiring artists and new music creators.
  • Enhance collaboration tools for music creators within the platform.
  • Introduce promotional capabilities for artists, providing a platform to showcase their work.

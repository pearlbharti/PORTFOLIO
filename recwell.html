SOLUTION #1

Sparse Visuals

  • Gamification of item swapping:To increase customer loyalty by giving points that they can use in store/online or have different badges for each level they reach and a special surprise on achieving it.
  • Alerting customers about new collection: To excite them and keep them in loop with the store in real time hence increasing a sense of personalization.
  • Having gift hampers at low costs: To give customers an occasion to thrift for, they would be able to make customized gift hampers and be able to give high quality yet affordable gifts to their loved ones, online.

The website lacks visually appealing elements such as high-quality product images, a vibrant color palette or engaging graphics, detracting from the overall fun and excitement of the thrifting experience.

SOLUTION #1

Limited shopping options

SOLUTION #3

Lacks a pull factor

adaptive approach to unexpected challenges

Our design team decided to put emphasis on the research and testing because of the complex nature of clients' workflow and service, the diverse user groups and lack of understanding of the design for seniors' accessibility. However, as our client changed functional requirements in the midway and the schedule was tight, we had to compromise on the time to spend on user testing. To make up for that, we decided to move parts of usability testing ahead to get feedback early while making adjustment on the function design.

Competitor Analysis

Spotify faces robust competition from industry heavyweights such as Apple Music, Amazon Music, YouTube Music, Tencent Music, and Pandora.

YouTube Music is grappling with slower digital space progress, Tencent Music dominates the Chinese market, and Pandora faces challenges in global expansion.

However, Spotify maintains a competitive edge through its extensive content library and aggressive user base expansion strategies, navigating the dynamic landscape of the music streaming industry.
